Rigatoni Bolognese
~1,100kcal
+
72g protein 110g carbs 38g fat
Ingredients
  • 5 oz rigatoni (dry)
  • 6 oz 80/20 ground beef
  • 1/2 cup crushed San Marzano tomatoes

Garlic Butter Salmon + Farro
~1,000kcal
+
70g protein 88g carbs 34g fat
Ingredients
  • 8 oz salmon fillet
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • Lemon, fresh dill or parsley
  • 1 cup dry farro

How: Cook farro like pasta (boil, drain). Sear salmon skin-side down 4 min, flip 2 min. In same pan, melt butter + garlic, pour over salmon. Squeeze lemon. Farro is nutty and chewy — nothing bland about it. Broccoli roasted at 425°F with olive oil + parmesan.

Gnocchi Aglio e Olio
~800kcal
+
28g protein 110g carbs 28g fat
Ingredients
  • 1 lb store-bought potato gnocchi
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 6 garlic cloves, thin sliced
  • Red chili flakes
  • Parmesan + fresh parsley
  • Salt + pasta water
How: Boil gnocchi (they float when done — 2-3 min). Meanwhile slowly toast garlic in olive oil until golden. Add chili flakes. Toss gnocchi in, splash of pasta water to emulsify. Parmesan + parsley to finish. This is one of Italy's simplest, most satisfying dishes.

Hitting 3,700 Calories — The Real Keys
  • Always cook with whole milk, not water. Always finish pasta with butter or olive oil. These invisible calories add up fast and you never taste "diet."
  • On 2-meal days, add a protein shake made with whole milk + 2 tbsp peanut butter. That's +600 kcal in 30 seconds.
  • Double batch dinners on Sunday or Wednesday — stuffed shells, bolognese, and meatballs all reheat perfectly. Saves time and keeps you on track.
  • Keep mixed nuts, granola, and peanut butter stocked at all times. These are your calorie insurance when life gets busy.
  • Farro and gnocchi are your secret weapons — they hit harder calorically than they seem and actually taste like something.
  • Your target weight of 175-180 is very achievable at your height. At 6'1", 190 lean is realistic long-term — but 175-180 athletic is a great first checkpoint.
